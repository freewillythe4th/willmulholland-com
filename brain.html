Questions

Asked at every workshop.

Which AI tool does it work with?

Claude and ChatGPT today. The brain is plain files, so when the next tool arrives it reads the same brain. Nothing to migrate.

Can my existing Notion be the brain?

Your Notion feeds it. The brain itself lives as files your AI tools read directly, so you keep Notion for what it is good at.

Do I need to code?

No. If you can write a doc, you can run this. Setup happens once, and in the cohort we do it together.

Where does my information live?

On your machine and in your own accounts. Nothing trains on your data, and you can take everything with you.

Who keeps the files updated?

Your normal work feeds it, that is the point of the workflows we set up together. The plumbing underneath is kept current for you.

Can my team share one brain?

Yes. Most people start solo in the cohort, then roll it out to the team once it is proving itself.

What is free?

The starter: the folder structure and setup guide to build a basic brain yourself. The cohort adds the guided build with your material, the live sessions, and the maintained skill library.

What happens to the skill library after the cohort?

You keep your brain and everything you built, full stop. Ongoing access to the maintained library is announced with the founding details.

How much time does it take?

One live session a week for four weeks, plus build time between sessions. You are building your own system as you go, so the hours land on your real work, not homework.

Why not just figure it out from YouTube?

You can, plenty do, slowly. The cohort exists for correct setup, feedback on your actual system, and troubleshooting when something breaks. You leave with it running, not with a watch-later list.
