01
The brain
Memory and retrieval. One context layer that reads all of it at once. The moat is here, not the model.
Writes in my voice, not generic AIEncoded writing voice · library of already-approved copy

An encoded writing voice is applied to everything and checked against copy already signed off, so what comes out reads like a person, not a generic AI draft.

Always works off the latest truthCore context doc rebuilt twice daily · self-updating skill files

One core document covering the live product, roadmap, people, decisions and priorities is rebuilt twice a day from the latest meetings, and each skill updates its own notes after every use, so the system sharpens over time instead of starting cold.

Everything ever learned is one search awayOne organised knowledge base · context loads itself for the task

Every project, person, decision and source lives in one place the system reads in full, with the relevant context pulled into whatever is being worked on.

02
Synthesis
The PMM craft chain. Intelligence becomes the launch, one ordered pass per feature.
research→ audience→ whitespace→ positioning→ messaging→ pricing→ GTM plan→ launch plan→ enablement
Gates over every draft: buyer-persona test · humaniser · fact-checker · refine-spec · council on high-stakes calls.
how the gates keep the bar high
Buyer-persona test. Every line checked against the real buyer's worldview and ban list before it ships.
Humaniser. Strips AI tells, kills the slop patterns, tightens to spoken rhythm.
Fact-checker. Claims verified against the source before they go out.
refine-spec. Pressure-tests the plan before anything gets built.
Council. A panel of reviewer agents that argues the high-stakes calls from different angles.
03
Outputs
One approved messaging doc fans out to every launch asset, in one voice.
GTM plan Sales one-pager Website updates Help article Blog Founder post Company-page post Messaging doc Lifecycle emails Battlecard Video walkthrough Asset for everyone to post
The engine that already works: elc-content-refresh turns one messaging doc into four derived assets in a single pass, with provenance and an approval gate. Generalising that fan-out to all of these is the build that turns the pile of skills into the system.
04
Distribution
Arm every team. Each person handed a ready-to-post asset, staged to approve then publish on launch day.
Marketing Sales Product Lifecycle Social Founder Creator team
Rails: Composio (LinkedIn / X / YouTube, stage then approve) · Braze (lifecycle email) · Webflow + Vercel (site) · HubSpot marketplace · Canva + Drive + Notion (collateral) · Slack (internal amplification).
05
Adoption + lifecycle
The launch is not done when it ships. It is done when people use the thing. Move the user from seeing it to adopting it.
Lifecycle email journeys Welcome + onboarding series Personalised by segment / ICP In-app triggers Paid-feature adoption nudges Expansion + retention prompts
The in-app layer: partner with lifecycle and product to design the moment that converts a user into an adopter of a paid surface. Running on email today, the personalised in-app trigger is the next build.
06
Measurement
Close the loop. Did it land. What moved adoption, what fell flat, and what the next launch should do differently.
Win/loss capture Launch retro Signal back into the brain Adoption + usage metrics Campaign-level attribution
The honest gap: today the loop closes by hand, a retro written back into the intelligence layer. Automating the metrics-to-brain feedback is build 03, so every launch genuinely starts smarter than the last.
↻

The loop closes. What you learn at adoption and measurement feeds straight back into intelligence in. The brain compounds. Every launch starts from a smarter place than the one before it.
