The shift

What being AI-native actually changed.

Same marketer. Same craft. A completely different reach.

01
I'm a product marketer, first and foremost.
I own positioning, messaging and launches end to end, and I work shoulder to shoulder with product, sales and design until the thing ships.
02
Then AI made me an AI-native marketer.
Same craft, now with code, live context and systems in reach. I stopped writing briefs for the work and started building the systems that do it.
03
Now every launch starts by talking to the data.
Sales calls, customer reviews, competitor ads and the product roadmap, read straight from the source instead of guessed at in a doc.
04
I pull company-wide context into every message.
Gong, Granola, review sites and the ad libraries, wired in through one brain, so the positioning is built on how buyers actually talk.
05
So I make sharper positioning calls, faster.
I spot the pattern and pressure-test the angle before I commit, instead of finding out at launch whether the story landed.
06
I think in whole launches, not just assets.
Positioning, messaging, GTM and every piece of content come out of one source, in one voice, so nothing leaks at the handoffs.
07
And I build the system that arms the whole team.
